    Course description.

    Topology is the study of properties of spaces and maps that are invariant under continuous deformation â€” the qualitative geometry captured by notions like continuity, convergence, compactness, and connectedness rather than by distances or angles. In this course we will build a rigorous foundation for these ideas and then use it as a common language for a range of modern examples, with particular attention to configuration spaces and moduli spaces, manifolds and their local-to-global structure, and profinite spaces that arise as inverse limits and in â€œarithmeticâ€ topological contexts. We will also introduce homology as a first bridge from point-set foundations to algebraic topology, using it to extract computable invariants and to illuminate how spaces are built. Along the way we will emphasize categorical perspectives â€” functoriality, universal properties, and adjointness â€” as tools for organizing constructions and revealing the relationships among these different kinds of spaces.
