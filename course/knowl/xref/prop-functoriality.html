Proposition 2.11.14. Functoriality of singular homology.
Singular homology is a functor from the category of topological spaces and continuous maps to the category of abelian groups and homomorphisms. Concretely:
🔗
(1) \((\mathrm{id}_X)_*=\mathrm{id}_{H_n(X)}\) for all \(n\text{.}\)
🔗
(2) \((g\circ f)_*=g_*\circ f_*\) for composable maps \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗
Proof.
Both properties follow immediately from the corresponding identities at the chain level: \((\mathrm{id}_X)_\sharp=\mathrm{id}_{C_n(X)}\) and \((g\circ f)_\sharp = g_\sharp\circ f_\sharp\) (since \((g\circ f)\circ\sigma = g\circ(f\circ\sigma)\)).
