Proposition 2.10.31. HEP via retractions.
A pair \((X,A)\) has the homotopy extension property if and only if \(X\times\{0\}\cup A\times[0,1]\) is a retract of \(X\times[0,1]\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗
Proof.
Write \(M_A := X\times\{0\}\cup A\times[0,1] \subseteq X\times[0,1]\) and let \(j\colon M_A\hookrightarrow X\times[0,1]\) denote the inclusion.
🔗
(\(\Leftarrow\)) Suppose \(r\colon X\times[0,1]\to M_A\) is a retraction, so \(r\circ j=\mathrm{id}_{M_A}\text{.}\) Given a map \(f\colon X\to Y\) and a homotopy \(h_A\colon A\times[0,1]\to Y\) with \(h_A(-,0)=f|_A\text{,}\) define \(g\colon M_A\to Y\) by \(g|_{X\times\{0\}}=f\) and \(g|_{A\times[0,1]}=h_A\text{;}\) these agree on the overlap \(A\times\{0\}\) since \(h_A(-,0)=f|_A\text{.}\) Then \(h:=g\circ r\colon X\times[0,1]\to Y\) is the desired extension: \(h(-,0)=g(r(-,0))=g(-,0)=f\) since \(r\) fixes \(X\times\{0\}\text{,}\) and \(h|_{A\times[0,1]}=g\circ r|_{A\times[0,1]}=g|_{A\times[0,1]}=h_A\) since \(r\) fixes \(M_A\text{.}\)
🔗
As a diagram, the construction is:

Diagram showing the retraction-based construction of the homotopy extension. The retraction \(r\) maps \(X\times[0,1]\) to \(M_A\text{,}\) and then \(g\) maps \(M_A\) to \(Y\text{.}\) The composite \(h=g\circ r\) gives the extension.
🔗
(\(\Rightarrow\)) Suppose \((X,A)\) has the HEP. Apply it with \(Y:=M_A\text{,}\) with \(f\colon X\to M_A\) given by \(f(x):=(x,0)\) (the inclusion of the base), and with \(h_A\colon A\times[0,1]\to M_A\) given by the identity \(h_A(a,t):=(a,t)\text{.}\) These are compatible since \(h_A(a,0)=(a,0)=f(a)\text{.}\) The HEP produces an extension \(r\colon X\times[0,1]\to M_A\text{,}\) and by construction \(r|_{M_A}=\mathrm{id}_{M_A}\text{,}\) so \(r\) is a retraction.
